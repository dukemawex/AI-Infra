NGO FIELD REPORT - GREAT LAKES REGION ENERGY SURVEY
Report ID: RPT-004
Date: 2023-10-31
Organization: Energy Without Borders - Rwanda/Burundi Program
Author: Survey Lead Pierre Nkurunziza

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
=================
Comprehensive energy infrastructure survey across Rwanda and Burundi,
covering 18 installations serving approximately 45,000 beneficiaries.

SITE ASSESSMENTS
================

Site 1: Kigali Northern Solar Hub (ASSET-0356)
Location: Kigali, -1.9403, 30.0618
Type: Solar System
Capacity: 35.0 kW
Current Status: operational
Installation Date: 2019-06-15
Last Maintenance: 2023-08-22
Condition Notes: Flagship installation for Rwanda Rural Electrification
Program. Running at 98% efficiency. Smart monitoring system transmits daily
reports. Battery storage upgraded in 2022. Zero downtime in past 18 months.
Community satisfaction survey score: 4.8/5.

Site 2: Musanze District Borehole (ASSET-0372)
Location: Musanze, -1.4989, 29.6349
Type: Borehole
Depth: 55 meters
Current Status: operational
Installation Date: 2016-09-30
Last Maintenance: 2023-04-12
Condition Notes: Well maintained by local water user association. Yield
stable at 3.8 m³/hour. Pump replaced preventively in 2022 before failure.
This site is an example of good community ownership.

Site 3: Bujumbura Peri-Urban Solar Array (ASSET-0389)
Location: Bujumbura, -3.3822, 29.3644
Type: Solar System
Capacity: 12.0 kW
Current Status: degraded
Installation Date: 2014-04-18
Last Maintenance: 2022-12-01
Condition Notes: Aging 9-year-old installation. Efficiency at 65%.
Multiple panels replaced last year. Inverter near end of life.
Vandalism has damaged 3 panels (repaired temporarily with tape - not
a long-term fix). Full system assessment and budget allocation needed.

Site 4: Lake Kivu Fishing Community Borehole (ASSET-0401)
Location: Rubavu, -1.6833, 29.2667
Type: Borehole
Depth: 42 meters
Current Status: offline
Installation Date: 2011-01-20
Last Maintenance: 2020-07-14
Condition Notes: Borehole has been non-functional since February 2023.
Suspected collapsed casing at 38-meter depth. No maintenance for 3+
years prior to failure. Community of 980 people now travels 4km to
nearest water source. Complete borehole rehabilitation or new borehole
required. Estimated cost: USD 22,000.

CROSS-BORDER COORDINATION NOTE
================================
Sites in Burundi show consistently higher degradation rates compared to
Rwanda, attributed to differences in community maintenance programs.
Recommend cross-border knowledge sharing initiatives.

Budget summary for next fiscal year:
- Emergency interventions: USD 35,000
- Preventive maintenance: USD 28,000
- Capacity building: USD 12,000
- Total: USD 75,000
