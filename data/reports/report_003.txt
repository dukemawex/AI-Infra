NGO FIELD REPORT - RURAL WATER INFRASTRUCTURE ASSESSMENT
Report ID: RPT-003
Date: 2023-07-05
Organization: WASH Tanzania
Author: Field Engineer Dr. Amina Hassan

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
=================
Assessment of water infrastructure in Dar es Salaam peri-urban zones and
Dodoma rural districts. Focus on aging boreholes installed pre-2012.

SITE ASSESSMENTS
================

Site 1: Temeke District Borehole Cluster (ASSET-0267)
Location: Dar es Salaam, -6.8235, 39.2695
Type: Borehole
Depth: 95 meters
Current Status: operational
Installation Date: 2009-03-22
Last Maintenance: 2023-01-18
Condition Notes: Despite age (14 years), system is well-maintained. Recent
rehabilitation in 2022 extended operational life. Yield: 4.2 m³/hour.
Community water committee active and functioning.

Site 2: Kinondoni Solar Pump Installation (ASSET-0289)
Location: Dar es Salaam, -6.7924, 39.2512
Type: Solar System
Capacity: 18.0 kW
Current Status: degraded
Installation Date: 2015-07-14
Last Maintenance: 2022-08-30
Condition Notes: Solar panels operating at 70% efficiency due to aging cells
(8-year-old panels nearing end of life). Controller unit replaced in 2022.
Output degradation accelerating. Full panel replacement recommended within
12 months. Estimated cost: USD 8,500.

Site 3: Dodoma Central Borehole (ASSET-0311)
Location: Dodoma, -6.1630, 35.7395
Type: Borehole
Depth: 88 meters
Current Status: critical
Installation Date: 2007-11-08
Last Maintenance: 2021-03-25
Condition Notes: Borehole is 16 years old and shows significant degradation.
Sand ingress increasing. Pump runs only 4 hours/day before overheating.
Casing integrity uncertain. Full borehole assessment required. Serving 3,200
people who have limited alternative water access. Urgent action required.

Site 4: Arusha Valley Solar Farm (ASSET-0334)
Location: Arusha, -3.3869, 36.6830
Type: Solar System
Capacity: 45.0 kW
Current Status: operational
Installation Date: 2020-02-28
Last Maintenance: 2023-05-10
Condition Notes: Relatively new installation performing excellently. Part of
government electrification program. Monitoring IoT sensors installed. System
expected to serve region for 15+ years without major intervention.

AGING INFRASTRUCTURE ANALYSIS
==============================
Of the 23 sites assessed this quarter, 8 are over 12 years old.
Older installations show disproportionate failure rates.
Recommended lifecycle replacement budget: USD 185,000 for next 24 months.
